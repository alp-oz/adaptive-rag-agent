ECB / EBA 2023 EU-Wide Stress Test — Summary Results
======================================================
Source: European Banking Authority, July 2023 (public domain)

OVERVIEW
--------
The 2023 EU-wide stress test covered 70 banks across the European Union,
representing approximately 75% of total banking sector assets. The exercise
assessed resilience under an adverse macroeconomic scenario projected over a
three-year horizon (2023–2025).

ADVERSE SCENARIO
----------------
The adverse scenario was designed by the European Systemic Risk Board (ESRB)
and assumed the following shocks:
  - EU GDP contraction of 6.0% cumulatively over three years
  - Unemployment rising by 6.1 percentage points to a peak of 12.2%
  - Residential real estate prices falling 21% and commercial real estate
    falling 29% cumulatively
  - A severe tightening of financial conditions with a 10-year sovereign
    yield increase of approximately 200 basis points

AGGREGATE CAPITAL RESULTS
--------------------------
Starting CET1 ratio (fully loaded, end-2022):    15.0%
CET1 ratio under adverse scenario (end-2025):    10.4%
Capital depletion (adverse scenario):             4.6 percentage points

Total capital ratio (starting point):            18.5%
Total capital ratio (adverse scenario):          13.7%

The aggregate CET1 ratio remains well above the minimum Pillar 1 requirement
of 4.5% and the 8% minimum total capital requirement under the adverse
scenario, indicating system-wide resilience.

LEVERAGE RATIO
--------------
Starting Tier 1 leverage ratio:                   5.5%
Tier 1 leverage ratio under adverse scenario:     4.2%

The leverage ratio also remains above the 3% minimum requirement across the
full adverse scenario horizon.

RISK-WEIGHTED ASSETS (RWA)
--------------------------
Aggregate RWA increase under adverse scenario:   +8.3%
Primary driver: credit risk migration and default in corporate and retail
loan portfolios under recessionary conditions.

Net interest income (NII) shows resilience in the early years due to the
rate environment, but deteriorates in 2024–2025 as credit losses accumulate.

CREDIT RISK
-----------
Cumulative net credit losses (adverse, 2023–2025):  €496 billion
  - Corporate exposures:    €211 billion
  - Retail exposures:       €178 billion
  - Institutions:            €42 billion
  - Other:                   €65 billion

Non-performing loan (NPL) ratio increases from 2.3% to 5.1% under the
adverse scenario, driven by downgrades in the corporate and SME segments.

MARKET RISK AND NII
-------------------
Net interest income declines cumulatively by €52 billion under the adverse
scenario, partly offset by improvements in net fee and commission income.
Net trading income losses amount to €27 billion under market risk shocks,
concentrated in banks with large trading book exposures.

BANK-LEVEL DISPERSION
---------------------
CET1 depletion ranges from 0.3 to 11.2 percentage points across individual
banks, reflecting heterogeneity in business models, geographic exposures, and
starting capital buffers. Banks with heavy reliance on commercial real estate
lending show the largest capital depletion.

No bank falls below the 4.5% CET1 minimum in the adverse scenario on a
fully-loaded basis, although several institutions show CET1 ratios between
5% and 7%, indicating limited headroom.

CAPITAL ADEQUACY REQUIREMENTS UNDER BASEL III
----------------------------------------------
Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1):       minimum 4.5% of RWA
Tier 1 capital:                     minimum 6.0% of RWA
Total capital ratio:                minimum 8.0% of RWA
Capital Conservation Buffer:        2.5% (must be met with CET1)
Countercyclical Capital Buffer:     0–2.5% (jurisdiction-specific)
G-SIB surcharge:                    1.0–3.5% for global systemically
                                    important banks

The fully-loaded CET1 ratio includes all Basel III transitional adjustments
applied in full. The transitional CET1 ratio, which phases in certain
deductions, is approximately 0.3–0.5 percentage points higher across the
sample.

LIQUIDITY
---------
The stress test does not directly assess liquidity positions, but supervisors
note that the Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) averaged 162% at end-2022,
well above the 100% minimum, and the Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR) averaged
127%, indicating strong short- and medium-term liquidity buffers.

CONCLUSION
----------
The 2023 EU-wide stress test demonstrates that the European banking system
has built up substantial capital buffers since the 2014–2016 stress testing
cycle, supported by supervisory pressure, retained earnings, and capital
issuance. However, significant tail risk remains in scenarios of prolonged
stagflation combined with sharp real estate corrections, particularly for
banks concentrated in commercial real estate and leveraged lending.

Supervisors will use the results to inform Pillar 2 capital requirements
in the 2023 Supervisory Review and Evaluation Process (SREP).
